## LinkedIn Post

One pricing mistake I see a lot: freelancers divide a salary goal by total working hours, then call that the hourly rate. That skips taxes, expenses, admin time, sales time, unpaid leave, and non-billable work. A better starting point is required annual revenue divided by realistic annual billable hours.

## Reddit Comment Without Link

A useful way to sanity-check the rate is to avoid using 40 billable hours as the baseline. Start with income target, expenses, tax planning buffer, and realistic billable hours after admin and sales time. If the number feels high, adjust scope or positioning before cutting the rate.
