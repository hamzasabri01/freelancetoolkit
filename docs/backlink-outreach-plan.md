# Backlink Outreach Plan

This plan is for the site owner. It is not public site copy and should not be exposed as a marketing claim.

## Goal

Build legitimate awareness for Freelance Work Tools by sharing useful calculators, guides, and the free freelance rate spreadsheet with communities that already discuss freelance pricing, billable hours, project estimates, and independent work.

## 30-Day Authority Plan

### Week 1: Prepare useful launch assets

- Publish and test the free freelance rate spreadsheet page.
- Confirm the download link works.
- Prepare one short demo showing how to calculate a sustainable freelance hourly rate.
- Collect 5-10 genuinely relevant communities, resource pages, newsletters, and blogs.
- Write one original article draft for Dev.to or a personal blog.

### Week 2: Helpful community posts

- Publish a Dev.to article: "How to Calculate a Freelance Hourly Rate Without Underpricing Yourself".
- Answer 2-3 relevant Quora questions with a helpful explanation and one natural link only where it fits.
- Share a LinkedIn post explaining the rate formula and linking to the spreadsheet.
- Post in Indie Hackers with the build story and ask for feedback, not backlinks.

### Week 3: Launch surfaces

- Prepare and submit a Product Hunt launch if the site is ready for public feedback.
- Share a Show HN post focused on usefulness and transparency.
- Ask for feedback from freelancers, developers, writers, designers, and consultants in communities where self-promotion is allowed.

### Week 4: Resource outreach

- Contact relevant blog owners, freelancer resource pages, and newsletter editors with a short personal note.
- Prioritize websites that already link to freelance calculators, rate guides, invoice templates, or spreadsheet resources.
- Track replies, mentions, and link placements manually.
- Update the spreadsheet page if feedback reveals missing context or unclear instructions.

## Safe Outreach Rules

- Do not buy links.
- Do not spam blog comments, forums, Discord servers, Slack groups, or social replies.
- Do not use private blog networks.
- Do not repeat the same anchor text everywhere.
- Do not submit to hundreds of low-quality directories.
- Do not claim fake users, fake revenue, fake testimonials, fake ratings, or fake partnerships.
- Prioritize useful content, real communities, and specific relevance.
- Use the brand name naturally: Freelance Work Tools.
- Use varied anchors such as "freelance rate spreadsheet", "freelance pricing calculator", "billable hours calculator", and "Freelance Work Tools" only where natural.
